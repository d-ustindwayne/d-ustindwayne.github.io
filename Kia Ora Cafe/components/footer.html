Frequently Asked Question

  1. Where does Kia Ora Cafe source its coffee beans?

    We import 100% of our coffee beans directly from sustainable farms in New Zealand, ensuring authentic flavor while supporting local communities.

  2. Do you offer dairy-free milk alternatives?

    Yes! We offer oat and almond milk at minimal charge for all our beverages.

  3. How many branches do you have?

    We currently have 11 branches across the Philippines, with our newest location opening soon in Auckland, New Zealand.

  4. Do I need experience to join your coffee courses?

    Not at all! We offer courses for all skill levels, from complete beginners to experienced baristas. Click here to view all of our available courses.

  5. Are your baked goods made in-house?

    Our pastries and sandwiches are prepared daily in each branch's kitchen using fresh, local ingredients.

  6. Can I host private events at your branches?

    Absolutely! Several of our branches offer private booking options for events, meetings, and gatherings. Contact us for availability.

  7. Can I borrow books from your in-cafe library?

    Yes! All our branches feature a small library section where customers can browse and read books while enjoying their coffee. We also offer free WiFi for up to 3 hours with any purchase.

  8. What makes Kia Ora different from other coffee shops?

    We uniquely blend authentic New Zealand coffee culture with Filipino hospitality, creating community spaces with books, games, and charitable initiatives.

  9. What's the story behind your website design?

    This website was inspired by Jasmine's dream coffee shop - a vision she shared with the developer long ago. It was built with love to bring her beautiful concept of a cultural bridge between New Zealand and the Philippines to life.

  10. Who developed the Kia Ora Cafe website?

    Created by Dustin Dwayne Diaz, a digital technology student at Manukau Institute of Technology. This website is a coded expression of Jasmine's inspiring dream to bridge cultures through coffee. For technical documentation, click here.
